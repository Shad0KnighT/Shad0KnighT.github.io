Studio C has a rich history that spans several milestones since it was created:

  • Established in 2003 by founders Matt Meese and Jared Shores
  • First aired on local TV stations in 2004
  • Joined national network in 2007
  • Reached 1 million subscribers on YouTube in 2013
  • Released first feature film "Studio C: The Movie" in 2017

Studio C was a popular comedy sketch television show that gained a dedicated fanbase for its clean humor, witty sketches, and talented cast. Originally created by Jared Shores and Matt Meese, Studio C was produced by BYUtv, a network owned by Brigham Young University. The show premiered on October 8, 2012, and quickly became a hit, especially among younger audiences and families looking for wholesome entertainment.

One of the defining characteristics of Studio C was its commitment to producing family-friendly comedy. Unlike many other sketch comedy shows, Studio C focused on humor that could be enjoyed by viewers of all ages without resorting to explicit content or profanity. This approach not only attracted a wide audience but also made it a favorite among parents looking for television shows they could watch with their children.

The show featured a regular cast of comedians who portrayed a wide range of characters and situations in their sketches. Some of the most memorable cast members included Matt Meese, Mallory Everton, Whitney Call, Jason Gray, Adam Berg, Stacey Harkey, Natalie Madsen, Stephen Meek, and Jeremy Warner. Each cast member brought a unique comedic style and personality to the show, contributing to its diverse and engaging content.

Studio C became known for its recurring characters and popular sketches, many of which became viral sensations online. Some of the most beloved recurring segments included "Scott Sterling," a hapless goalkeeper who unintentionally saves goals with his face, and "The Awkward Avoidance Viking," a character who intervenes in uncomfortable social situations by offering bizarre and hilarious solutions.

In addition to its sketches, Studio C also featured musical performances and guest appearances from other notable comedians and entertainers. The show's format evolved over its run, incorporating fan feedback and experimenting with different comedic styles and themes while maintaining its core principles of clean humor and entertainment suitable for all ages.

The popularity of Studio C extended beyond television, with the show amassing a significant online following through platforms like YouTube. Many of Studio C's sketches garnered millions of views, further cementing its status as a cultural phenomenon in the realm of online comedy.

After several successful seasons, Studio C underwent changes in its lineup and format. In 2018, some of the original cast members departed to pursue other projects, leading to the creation of a new sketch comedy group called JK! Studios. This spin-off group continued to produce comedy content in a similar vein to Studio C, maintaining the same commitment to clean humor and entertainment.

Overall, Studio C left a lasting impact on the comedy landscape, demonstrating that humor can be both hilarious and wholesome. Its success paved the way for other family-friendly comedy shows and showcased the enduring appeal of clean entertainment in an era often dominated by more mature and explicit content.
